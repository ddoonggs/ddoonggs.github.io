Introduction¶

Statistical analysis is becoming more and more important in many sports including basketball. Teams are organizing analytics teams in their organization and makes many in-game and off-the-court decisions based on the data provided. This project is an attempt to visualize the analytics being used in the NBA and to derive interesting relationships between variables that are in the game of basketball.¶

One big difference between the current NBA and the NBA of Michael Jordan's time is the use of the 3-point shot. Since the appearence of Stephen Curry in the NBA in 2009, the use of the 3-point shot has increased significantly. With NBA teams changing offensive tactics to be more focused on the 3-point shot, we make the assumption that the 3-point shot has become more important in winning a NBA game than before. The sections below will build up to figure out if the previous statement holds true to a certain degree.¶

This article in this link explains the impact of Stephen Curry and how the 3-point shot has changed the NBA.¶

Data Collection¶

We will be gathering two datasets.¶

  • League average stats from 2000-2018 in each year.
  • NBA team average stats from 2000-2018 in each year

For the first dataset, we will be web-scraping with the help of the urllib and BeautifulSoup. Prior Knowledge is expected. This dataset will be used during the visualization sections of the project. Link to website¶

We want to gather information about the 3-point shot and the 2-point shot. Since the data from the selected website does contain stats related to the 2-point shot, we will calculated them with the following formulas.¶

  1. 2PA = FGA - 3PA
  2. 2PM = FGM - 3PM
  3. 2P% = 2PM/2PA

Abbreviations ->

             3PA = 3-point shot attempts
             3PM = Made 3-point shots
             3P% = Made 3-point shot percentage(3PM/3PA)
             2PA = 2-point shot attempts
             2PM = Made 2-point shots
             2P% = Made 2-point shot percentage(2PM/2PA)
             FGA = Field Goal attempts
             FGM - Made Field Goals
             FG% = Made field goals percentage(FGM/FGA)

for x in range(1,22):
  cols = rows[x].find_all("td")
  if not cols:
    continue
  else:
    year = year-1
    years.append(year)
    attempts3 = str(cols[10].renderContents())
    attempts3 = float(attempts3[2:-1])
    three_points_attempt.append(attempts3)
    mades3 = str(cols[9].renderContents())
    mades3 = float(mades3[2:-1])
    three_points_made.append(mades3)
    percents3 = str(cols[23].renderContents())
    percents3 = float(percents3[2:-1])
    three_points_percentage.append(percents3)
    fga = str(cols[8].renderContents())
    fga  = float(fga[2:-1])

    attempts2 = fga-attempts3
    mades2 = fgm-mades3
    percents2 = mades2/attempts2
    two_points_attempt.append(attempts2)
    two_points_made.append(mades2)
    two_points_percentage.append(percents2)

Analysis¶

In the analysis section of this project, we will use machine learning to find out the correlation between the 3,2 point shots and win rate in the NBA. A linear regression algorithem will be used to produce a predictive model which we will use to see if certain variables relating to the 3,2 point shots have a linear relationship with win rate. Here we will use the two datasets we've organized from Kaggle. By running linear regression on both the data from the "2000-2008" period and "2009-2018" period, we can see if winning basketball in the "Stephen Curry's 3-point shot era" really is related much with the 3-point shot.¶

winRates = [[y] for y in firstHalf['WIN%'].values]

Insight¶

What have we learned from the series of processes starting from data collection to analysis using linear aggression? In the visualization step, we plotted series of [time vs 3,2 point shot data] graphs. Here we saw a reduce in popularity of the 2-point shot and an increase in popularity of the 3-point shot. Also, the accuracy of the 2-point shot increased and the accuracy of the 3-point shot has no outstanding trend. This can be explained by how NBA players do not take 2-point shots that are "long" or shot close to the 3-point line. This means more 2-point shots are attempted near the basketball which means a higher accuracy of the 2-point shot. This trend of avoiding the long 2-point shot is explained and visualized in this article. link¶

Now what insight did we gain from the analysis step using linear regression? In this section we found that the highest difference in R-squared value occured with 3-point shot accuracy data. This can be translated to the statement ,"Out of the 6 variables(3pa, 3pm, 3p%, 2pa, 2pm, 2p%) the variable that has increased its influence on the win rate by most over time is 3-point shot accuracy." This statement aligns well with the earlier statement we made in the introduction of how the NBA has changed and how the 3-point shot became more important in winning.¶
